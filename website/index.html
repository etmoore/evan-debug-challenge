Evan Moore

Full Stack Instructor

Developer

In March 2014, I quit my job to chase my dream of becoming a developer. Since then, I completed a three month web development course at Front Range Community College, interned at Digital Globe as a front-end developer, and graduated from gSchool a full-stack Ruby and Javascript developer. I am now an instructor in Galvanize's Full Stack program and couldn't be more excited to help others discover the joy of web development.

Entrepreneur

Between Oct '12 and Jan '14, I founded and ran a startup called TeacherMaps, a web application (built in Rails) that allows teachers to create, organize, and share instructional materials with other educators around the country. My two co-founders and I achieved the following before amicably parting ways: First Place, CU New Venture Challenge; First Place, Startup Weekend EDU; Semi-finalist, Penn Graduate School of Education Business Plan Competition. This experience taught me a lot about myself and is what ultimately convinced me to quit my job to become a developer.

Educator

After college, I spent 3 incredibly rewarding years teaching middle school science as a Teach For America corps member. In 2012 I left the classroom and moved to Colorado with my wife where I spent the first summer working on school finance policy (Amendment 66) with State Senator Mike Johnston. I then spent a year and a half (Sept '12 - March '14) as the Assistant Director and later Director of Education Services at Voqal where I advised the governing board on education strategy and helped to kickstart the Crash Course US Government Youtube Series. Through these experiences I developed a passion for education, particularly edtech and the art of instruction.
